The Niscomed 5 LPM Single Flow is the cost-reduced cousin of the Niscomed 5 LPM Dual Flow — same physical chassis, same power architecture, single output instead of dual. At ₹42,240 per the manufacturer brochure and e-commerce product listings, the Niscomed Single Flow sits in the direct price overlap with the Keyhub 5 LPM, which is priced identically at ₹42,240. The two machines are legitimate head-to-head competitors at exactly the same price point, and the comparison is not flattering for the Niscomed. Where the Keyhub declares CE certification and two alarms, the Niscomed declares only one alarm and no certifications. Where the Keyhub is 16 kg, the Niscomed is 25 kg. The Niscomed's only meaningful advantage is the 10 psi outlet pressure, which is competitive but not dispositive.

## What the specs actually mean in Indian homes

The Niscomed 5 LPM Single Flow delivers 90-95% oxygen purity at a continuous flow range of 1 to 5 LPM [CITATION: Niscomed Single Flow product brochure]. The 1 LPM floor excludes paediatric and very-low-flow adult prescriptions — the Keyhub at the same price covers 0.5 LPM. For nocturnal-only domiciliary oxygen patients with stable COPD titrated at 0.5-1 LPM, the Niscomed's 1 LPM floor means the machine cannot deliver the lowest prescribed dose accurately. Patients above 1 LPM are not affected.

Weight is 25 kg — the same chassis as the dual-flow sibling but without the second outlet and flow meter. This is significantly heavier than every 5 LPM competitor at this price point. The Keyhub 5 at 16 kg is 9 kg lighter; the Fitmate 5 at 18 kg is 7 kg lighter; the Healthgenie 5 at 21 kg is 4 kg lighter. The 25 kg reflects an older-generation industrial chassis architecture. For fixed installation in one room this is a one-time setup issue; for routine inter-room shifting it is impractical.

Power consumption of 530 W is high for a 5 LPM output. Most competent 5 LPM PSA units run at 300-390 W for the same flow rate. The Niscomed's 530 W suggests an overbuilt compressor capacity that is not delivering proportional output — possibly because the unit shares core components with the 10 LPM variant. At Indian domestic tariff of ₹8-10 per kWh, 530 W continuous is ₹102-128 per day or ₹37,000-46,000 per year at 24/7. Over a 3-year service life at 12 hours/day this is ₹65,000-80,000 in electricity — nearly 2x the cost of running a 300 W Oxybliss 5 LPM over the same period. The running-cost penalty of the Niscomed alone justifies paying a price premium for any efficient competitor.

Noise at 50 dB is middle-of-the-pack. Quieter than Fitmate 5 (55 dB) and Healthgenie (55 dB); louder than Eloxy, Oxybliss, and Equinox at 40 dB; louder than Keyhub at 48 dB. For bedroom placement this is borderline. For living-room daytime use it is acceptable.

Outlet pressure of 10 psi is the Niscomed's genuine strength. This is higher than the Fitmate 5 (7.25 psi), the Keyhub (13 psi — Keyhub wins), the Eloxy (10 psi — tied), and the Philips EverFlo (5-7 psi). For extended tubing runs, for humidifier setups, and for downstream device compatibility the 10 psi is a material advantage.

Dimensions of 23.6 × 14.7 × 14.3 inches are standard for the chassis class.

Compliance: Loss of Power Alarm declared Yes. System Malfunction Alarm blank. No Flow Alarm blank. US FDA blank. FAA blank. CE blank. Indian Voltage Model declared Yes [CITATION: Niscomed Single Flow compliance declarations]. This is the single-alarm, zero-certification pattern. The Niscomed Single Flow has more declared content than the Dual Flow (which had zero alarms) but less than the Keyhub (which has two alarms and CE).

## Who should buy the Niscomed 5 LPM Single Flow

The defensible buyer profile is very narrow: someone who specifically needs 10 psi outlet pressure (extended tubing runs, humidifier intensive use) at a ₹42,000 budget and cannot stretch to Keyhub pricing (which is identical at ₹42,240, so this constraint does not actually exist in practice). In real terms, there is no rational buyer profile where the Niscomed Single Flow is preferred over the Keyhub at the same price — the Keyhub wins on weight, compliance, no-flow alarm coverage, power efficiency, and outlet pressure parity. The Niscomed Single Flow is only a defensible pick if Keyhub is out of stock and the buyer has immediate need.

## Who should not buy the Niscomed 5 LPM Single Flow

Buyers who have access to a Keyhub 5 LPM at the same price. Buyers with paediatric prescriptions needing 0.5 LPM. Buyers planning bedroom placement — 50 dB is marginal. Buyers concerned with running cost — 530 W is penalising. Buyers at tier-2 cities — no declared national service network. Life-dependent patients — no-flow alarm is absent.

## How it compares: Niscomed Single Flow vs Keyhub vs Oxybliss vs Yuwell 7F

**Niscomed Single Flow vs Keyhub 5 LPM** — Identical price at ₹42,240. Keyhub has CE declared, loss-of-power plus no-flow alarms, 16 kg weight (vs 25 kg), 320 W power (vs 530 W), 48 dB noise (vs 50 dB), 13 psi outlet pressure (vs 10 psi), 0.5 LPM flow floor (vs 1 LPM). Across every dimension the Keyhub wins. Verdict: Keyhub wins at identical price by a wide margin — Niscomed Single Flow is not rational.

**Niscomed Single Flow vs Oxybliss 5 LPM** — Oxybliss is listed at ₹36,480 (₹5,760 cheaper). Oxybliss is lighter (not declared but similar physical dimensions), 40 dB noise (10 dB quieter), 300 W power (significantly lower), 0.5 LPM flow floor. Both have blank compliance rows; Oxybliss has all alarms blank while Niscomed has loss-of-power declared. At lower price Oxybliss is materially better except for the declared loss-of-power alarm. Verdict: Oxybliss wins on cost and efficiency; Niscomed only wins for buyers who require the declared loss-of-power alarm.

**Niscomed Single Flow vs Yuwell 7F** — Yuwell 7F at ₹45,000-55,000 (₹2,760-12,760 more expensive). Full alarm coverage (power, malfunction, no-flow), Yuwell's Chinese national medical device registration, Indian service network in 40+ cities, 14-15 kg weight (10 kg lighter), 43 dB noise (quieter), 300-350 W power (more efficient), 0.5 LPM flow floor. Yuwell 7F wins across every specification. Verdict: Yuwell 7F wins decisively for buyers with even minimal budget flexibility.

## Indian-market considerations

The Niscomed brand's broader presence in Indian retail (BP monitors, nebulisers, health accessories) creates consumer recognition that does not extend to clinical-grade concentrator support. The same caveat as with Healthgenie: brand recognition in consumer health accessories does not translate to medical device service infrastructure.

Power infrastructure: 530 W requires a 2 kVA stabiliser (₹5,000-8,000). This is more expensive stabiliser infrastructure than a 350 W Keyhub requires (1 kVA at ₹3,000-4,500). The Niscomed's higher power draw adds roughly ₹2,500 in up-front infrastructure cost plus the ongoing electricity premium.

CDSCO notification: not declared in the public Niscomed Single Flow listing [CITATION: CDSCO notification database]. For a ₹42,240 medical device purchase the buyer should demand the notification number.

Service network: no declared national network. Warranty through importing distributor. Parts turnaround 4-8 weeks for major components.

Stock status on primary listing is Out of stock. This is not the same as Discontinued (which is the Dual Flow variant's status) but may indicate supply-chain issues or transition to a newer model. Buyers should verify current-batch availability with the dealer.

## Verdict

The Niscomed 5 LPM Single Flow exists in an awkward position. It is cheaper than certified branded units (Philips, Yuwell, Nidek) but more expensive and worse-specified than equivalent-priced unbranded competitors (Keyhub). It is not bad enough to be deeply dangerous like its sibling Dual Flow (which has zero declared alarms) but not good enough to be rational at its price point. For any buyer with ₹42,240 to spend on a 5 LPM, the Keyhub at the same price is better in every measurable dimension. For any buyer willing to stretch to ₹45,000-55,000, the Yuwell 7F is dramatically better. The Niscomed Single Flow has no specific use case where it outperforms alternatives at similar budgets. Score: 5.0 out of 10.
