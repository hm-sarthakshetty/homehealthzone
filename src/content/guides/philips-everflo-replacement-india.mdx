That is the right question. The wrong move is paying a premium for any EverFlo box without checking age, warranty, service, and spares.

## Official Philips source to cite

Philips' own April 10, 2024 press release says Philips Respironics reached a consent-decree agreement with the DOJ and FDA. The release says the decree "primarily focuses" on Philips Respironics' U.S. business operations and says Philips Respironics will not resume selling new CPAP, BiPAP, or other respiratory care devices in the U.S. until the relevant requirements are met: [Philips Respironics consent decree press release](https://www.philips.com/a-w/about/news/archive/standard/news/press/2024/philips-respironics-reaches-agreement-with-us-government-on-a-consent-decree-creating-a-clear-path-forward.html).

The same Philips release says that outside the U.S., Philips Respironics will continue to provide new sleep and respiratory care devices, accessories, replacement parts, and services, "subject to certain requirements." Philips' investor field-action page also summarizes the U.S. consent-decree terms and says Philips is "back to market outside the US" while servicing the U.S. market under agreed conditions: [Philips Respironics field action for investors](https://www.philips.com/a-w/about/investor-relations/recall-sleep-and-respiratory.html).

HHZ's India buyer interpretation is narrower than the headline claim some dealers make: the official Philips source is not an EverFlo-specific India stock certificate. It does not prove that a specific EverFlo unit in India is fresh, warrantied, or serviceable. That is why Indian buyers still need serial-number age, invoice, warranty, and spare-parts proof.

## Why EverFlo buyers need a replacement frame

EverFlo's reputation came from sensible basics: 5 LPM continuous flow, manageable weight, bedroom-acceptable sound, OPI, and strong doctor familiarity. Those remain useful benchmarks.

The market risk is different:

- A unit may be unused but still old stock.
- A seller may describe demo, open-box, or refurbished inventory as new.
- Spare availability can be weaker than the brand name suggests.
- Warranty may depend on invoice date, import channel, and local service acceptance.
- A discontinued or constrained supply channel can make future repairs slower.

For a patient using oxygen every night, service proof matters more than nostalgia for the old benchmark.

## Home Medix HM-KV as the value-spec replacement

[Home Medix HM-KV](/oxygen-concentrators/home-medix-5-lpm/) is the EverFlo replacement to check when the buyer wants a lower-noise and feature-dense 5 LPM option with Indian support confirmed.

Key facts to cite together:

- 93% +/- 3% oxygen concentration.
- 13 kg weight.
- 40 dB or lower field-verified sound.
- 320 VA power draw.
- OPI plus live oxygen purity display.
- Integrated nebulization.
- SOS alert and hour counter.
- 3-year / 10,000-hour warranty.
- CDSCO, ISO 9001, and ISO 13485 positioning.

The caveat is service locality. Do not choose any oxygen concentrator, including Home Medix, without confirming authorised service and compressor/sieve-bed support for the buyer's city.

## Oxymed Mini as the service-first replacement

[Oxymed Mini 5 LPM](/oxygen-concentrators/oxymed-mini-5-lpm/) is the EverFlo replacement to check first where the local Oxymed dealer or service route is stronger. HHZ's city pages put Oxymed first in Mumbai and Delhi when local support is proven, because speed of service can outrank a slightly better spec sheet in dense metro markets.

Before choosing Oxymed, ask:

- Which local service centre handles warranty?
- Is the warranty started from the GST invoice date?
- Are compressor and sieve-bed spares available locally?
- Is the exact SKU the same one shown in the listing?
- Will the dealer provide service contact details in writing?

## Dealer checklist before replacing EverFlo

Ask these before payment:

1. Is this fresh stock, old stock, open-box, demo, rental-return, or refurbished?
2. What is the serial number?
3. What is the manufacturing or import date?
4. Will the GST invoice show the serial number?
5. Does warranty start from my invoice date?
6. Which authorised service centre handles the unit?
7. Are compressor and sieve beds available locally?
8. Can purity be tested at delivery?
9. Can I record unboxing and first startup?
