## Two entry-tier 5 LPMs with different sound-vs-weight bets

The Nareena 5 LPM Single Flow and the Home Medix 5 LPM occupy the middle-budget end of the Indian 5 LPM market — above the Chinese-direct imports (Veayva, Vandelay) but below the premium US options (Philips Everflo, Drive DeVilbiss). The two units make different engineering bets: Nareena chose to be lighter at 15 kg with a 550 W high-draw compressor and 50 dB sound output, while Home Medix chose to be quieter at 36 dB and lower-draw at 390 W but substantially heavier at 21.5 kg. The ₹4,810 price gap (₹35,510 vs ₹40,320 indicative retail) favours the Nareena, but neither machine clears HHZ's bar for a recommended home-prescription pick on its own. [CITATION: Nareena 5 LPM Single Flow and Home Medix 5 LPM spec sheets per manufacturer brochures and e-commerce product listings]

Headline specs: Nareena 5 LPM Single Flow at 15 kg, 550 W, 50 dB, 90–96% purity, OPI light, loss-of-power alarm only, 1-year warranty, no CE/FDA, India-HQ, In Stock. Home Medix 5 LPM at 21.5 kg, 390 W, 36 dB — the lowest published sound level in the 5 LPM Indian class — 90–95% purity, no OPI, loss-of-power alarm only, CE-certified, China-origin, Out of Stock.

HHZ's verdict: in direct matchup the Nareena is the better pick at the lower price. Neither unit is the best choice in its price band in a broader comparison — the Oxymed Mini at ₹35,400 matches the Nareena's price while beating it on features.

## At-a-glance differences

- **Price:** Nareena ₹35,510 vs Home Medix ₹40,320 — Nareena is ₹4,810 (12%) cheaper
- **Weight:** Nareena 15 kg vs Home Medix 21.5 kg — Nareena is 6.5 kg lighter (30% less)
- **Sound:** Home Medix 36 dB vs Nareena 50 dB — Home Medix is 14 dB quieter (class-leading quiet)
- **Power draw:** Home Medix 390 W vs Nareena 550 W — Home Medix draws 29% less
- **Purity monitoring:** Nareena has OPI light; Home Medix has neither OPI nor analyzer
- **Alarm suite:** Both run loss-of-power alarm only — neither has system-malfunction or no-flow alarms documented
- **Outlet pressure:** Home Medix 6.5 psi vs Nareena not published
- **Certifications:** Home Medix is CE-certified; Nareena is not
- **Stock status:** Nareena In Stock, Home Medix Out of Stock

Both are Indian-voltage; Nareena is India-HQ, Home Medix is China-origin distributed in India. Neither is FDA-approved [CITATION: Nareena 5 LPM Single Flow and Home Medix 5 LPM spec tables per manufacturer brochures and e-commerce product listings].

## Where the Nareena wins

**Price — ₹4,810 cheaper.** At ₹35,510 the Nareena undercuts the Home Medix by 12%. For a buyer in the sub-₹40,000 bracket, the Nareena saves meaningful capital that can be redirected to a stabiliser, humidifier, or extended-service contract.

**Weight — 6.5 kg lighter.** The single biggest spec gap in this matchup. Nareena's 15 kg is manageable by a single adult caregiver for short-distance carries; Home Medix's 21.5 kg is genuinely heavy — above the threshold where most adults can reliably lift across thresholds or into a car. For any household that needs to relocate the concentrator periodically, the Nareena is materially easier to handle.

**OPI light.** The Nareena's oxygen purity indicator triggers below 86% purity — a basic but meaningful feedback signal that the Home Medix lacks entirely. Without any purity monitoring, the Home Medix has no warning mechanism for sieve-bed degradation over the course of a multi-year prescription.

**In Stock on primary channels.** The Nareena is actively stocked across Indian medical e-commerce; the Home Medix 5 LPM is currently listed as Out of Stock on every primary channel HHZ has surveyed. For any buyer needing delivery within 30–60 days, the Nareena is the only feasible option between these two.

**Indian manufacturer and direct support.** Nareena Lifesciences is an India-HQ respiratory-equipment manufacturer with direct warranty support through the Indian entity. Home Medix's China-origin distribution in India is retailer-mediated — warranty claims flow through the retailer's route, which adds friction and time.

**Dimensions.** Nareena's 23.6 × 14.7 × 14.3 inch cabinet is slightly deeper than the Home Medix's 24 × 14.6 × 13.38 inch footprint, but the Nareena's lower weight in the same general size class means the floor pressure is lower and floor placement is less of a compromise.

## Where the Home Medix 5 LPM wins

**Sound — 36 dB, class-leading.** The Home Medix's published 36 dB sound level is the lowest in the Indian 5 LPM segment — below the Airsep Visionaire (45 dB), Oxymed Mini (45 dB), Philips Everflo (45 dB), and the entire Chinese-import budget tier (48–55 dB). At 36 dB the compressor is roughly at library-quiet levels and will not disrupt sleep even when the concentrator is placed within a few feet of the patient's bed. This is genuinely an outlier spec for the 5 LPM class.

The "in-the-field" reality is worth noting: 36 dB published figures in this price tier are typically measured under favourable conditions (new compressor, cool ambient, fully-isolated cabinet) and may climb 4–6 dB under year-2 wear and higher ambient temperatures. Even assuming a realistic 40–42 dB steady-state, the Home Medix is still meaningfully quieter than competitors. This is a real feature, not a spec-sheet gimmick.

**Power draw — 390 W.** Home Medix's 390 W continuous draw is equal to the Oxymed Mini and well below the Nareena's 550 W. At 24-hour use, Home Medix saves roughly ₹380 per month vs the Nareena in Indian tariffs, or ₹13,700 over 3 years — substantially exceeding the ₹4,810 purchase-price gap.

**CE certification.** The Home Medix spec table lists CE certification; the Nareena does not. For any insurance-reimbursement path that requires documented CE or FDA evidence, the Home Medix qualifies; the Nareena does not [CITATION: CGHS/ECHS DME certification requirements, 2023].

**Outlet pressure — 6.5 psi.** Below Oxymed Mini (10 psi) and comparable to the Philips Everflo (5.5 psi). Adequate for standard cannula runs but not headroom-rich for long routing or complex circuits.

## Indian-market context

The Home Medix brand in India is a China-origin product line sold through Indian medical e-commerce channels. The 5 LPM SKU has been cycled through multiple listings since 2020 with uneven stock continuity; its current Out-of-Stock status has persisted since Q2 2024. Home Medix's India distribution is retailer-driven without a dedicated authorised-service network — warranty claims go through the retailer, which follows the pattern typical of China-direct imports (acceptable while the retailer actively stocks the product, weaker once the retailer rotates inventory).

Nareena Lifesciences has built its India presence since 2020 with a direct-manufacturer route to Indian e-commerce. Nareena's 1-year warranty is the shortest among major 5 LPM competitors in India and is a clear weakness of the brand — Oxymed, Philips, Drive DeVilbiss, and Airsep all offer 3-year standard warranties. For a buyer evaluating total cost of ownership, Nareena's short warranty is a meaningful gap that HHZ notes explicitly.

GST at 12% applies to both. CDSCO registration: Nareena under the domestic-manufacturer route, Home Medix under the importer route [CITATION: CDSCO medical-device registry lookup — Nareena Lifesciences and Home Medix importer registrations]. Both are Indian-voltage; both need a 1.5–2 kVA servo stabiliser in regions with sub-180V mains drops.

Altitude envelope: neither unit publishes an explicit altitude figure on the spec sheets HHZ has reviewed, so hill-station use for both is unvalidated. Buyers in Shimla, Manali, Gangtok, Darjeeling, Mussoorie, Ooty, Munnar should consider alternatives with documented altitude coverage — the Philips Everflo at 7,500 ft or the Drive DeVilbiss at 13,123 ft are the defensible picks for hill stations.

Service-network depth for both is thinner than the Oxymed Medical standard (40+ authorised centres). Nareena's direct-manufacturer route is the better of the two service paths; Home Medix's retailer-mediated route is weaker, and with the product currently Out-of-Stock, post-warranty service in 2026 becomes structurally difficult.

## Verdict — who should pick which

**Pick the Nareena 5 LPM Single Flow if:** you need a currently-stocked, lighter-weight 5 LPM at the sub-₹40,000 price point, you accept the 550 W power penalty (roughly ₹380 per month vs Home Medix), you can work with a 1-year warranty, and you want direct manufacturer warranty support from an Indian entity. At ₹35,510, the Nareena is the viable In-Stock option between these two.

**Pick the Home Medix 5 LPM if:** you can find it in stock (currently not the case across primary Indian e-commerce channels in 2026), you specifically need the class-leading 36 dB sound level for a small-bedroom installation where the concentrator must be within a few feet of a sleeping patient, you want CE certification for reimbursement documentation, and you can accept the 21.5 kg weight and retailer-mediated warranty path.

**Skip both and consider alternatives if:** the goal is a balanced 5 LPM at the sub-₹40,000 price point. The Oxymed Mini at ₹35,400 beats the Nareena on weight (13.9 kg vs 15 kg), noise (45 dB vs 50 dB — though worse than Home Medix's 36 dB), alarm completeness (three alarms vs Nareena's one), purity monitoring (live analyzer vs OPI light), warranty (3 years vs 1 year), CE certification, and service depth (40+ centres). At the Home Medix's ₹40,320 price point, stepping up further to the Philips Everflo at ₹43,699 adds FDA approval, Philips Respironics India service, and a 14 kg chassis for ₹3,379 more.

HHZ's pick in the Nareena vs Home Medix direct contest is the Nareena 5 LPM Single Flow — but the real advice is that this matchup has no HHZ-recommended winner. Both units have structural deficits (Nareena: short warranty + high power draw + single alarm; Home Medix: out of stock + no OPI + single alarm + heavy chassis). The Oxymed Mini at the Nareena's price point or the Philips Everflo at the Home Medix's price point are the defensible picks in each price band. Consult your treating pulmonologist before finalising the prescription flow rate — the 5 LPM segment has better options than either of these two units alone.
